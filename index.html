Speech Graphs

Paste annotated speech and render the sequence of entities as a graph.

Purpose

Use this page to inspect how annotated entities recur and transition through a speech sample. By now, natural language is ignored; only entity labels like [Noun Phrase]1 are parsed.

How to annotate

Wrap each mention in brackets and add its entity index immediately after it: [she]1, [the school]3. Consecutive mentions create directed edges.

Annotated text

Maximum 1000 words. Up to 20 renders are allowed per browser session.

Graph

Node size reflects recurrence. Arrow direction follows the mention sequence.

Project reference

This project is inspired by previous work on coreference and discourse organization in psychosis, Coreference delays in psychotic discourse: widening the temporal window . The study examined how speakers with psychotic disorders introduce and maintain references to people, objects, and events across discourse, showing that referential links may span longer temporal distances than traditionally assumed. These findings highlight the importance of tracking entities beyond adjacent sentences and motivate the development of tools that make coreference structures explicit and measurable. The present project extends this line of research by providing an interactive environment for annotating and visualizing discourse entities and their referential connections, facilitating the exploration of how meaning is maintained, shifted, or disrupted throughout a text.


Cite as: Palominos, C., Figueroa-Barra, A., & Hinzen, W. (2023). Coreference delays in psychotic discourse: widening the temporal window. Schizophrenia Bulletin, 49(Supplement_2), S153–S162.
